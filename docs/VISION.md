# Vision — Spot the Bot

## The problem

"Guess the AI" quizzes are everywhere, and almost all of them are static: a hardcoded
list of ten examples written once, shipped, and never touched again. They're funny for a
week and then stale, because the model landscape moves faster than any hand-curated
example set can track. By the time you've memorized the tells of last year's model, the
quiz hasn't updated to test you against this year's.

## Who it's for

Anyone who reads AI-generated text often enough to have an opinion about whether they can
spot it — which, in 2026, is almost everyone. No account, no install: open the link, play
a round in under two minutes, share the score.

## The core idea

Ten short passages, one at a time. Tap **human** or **AI**. No skipping, no going back.
After round ten, a reveal: your score, which passages fooled you, and — the payoff —
whether you got fooled by the exact model that's topical *this week*. The quiz is only
interesting because the content underneath it refreshes; the UI is deliberately simple so
the passage bank is what carries the product.

The passage bank is generated on a schedule (see the content-pipeline epic in
`docs/BACKLOG.md`) by prompting the current frontier model for short passages across a
mix of everyday styles — news lede, product review, diary entry, recipe intro — and
pairing each with a length- and topic-matched human-written passage. Old banks are kept
so a given week's round always draws from a bank no older than that week.

## Key design decisions

- **Static site, no backend.** The passage bank is a versioned JSON artifact generated by
  a build-time script, not a live API call. This keeps hosting free and the game
  playable offline once loaded, and matches the "servable" requirement of shipping to
  `apps.charliekrug.com/spot-the-bot` with no server.
- **One passage at a time, no backtracking.** Mirrors the read of a real skim — you don't
  get to compare passages side by side, because that's not how you'd encounter this text
  in the wild.
- **The reveal names the model, not just "AI."** Calling out *which* model fooled you
  (when the pipeline has that metadata) is the actual hook — "you got fooled by the model
  everyone's talking about this week" is shareable in a way that a bare percentage isn't.
- **Weekly cadence, not daily.** Frontier model releases and news cycles move on a weekly
  rhythm, not a daily one; a daily refresh would mostly reshuffle the same underlying
  model with no new signal.
- **Content pipeline is a first-class part of the product**, not a backstage script. It's
  the thing that makes this different from a static quiz, so it's scoped and tested with
  the same rigor as the UI (see Epic 2 in the backlog).

## What "v1 done" looks like

- The ten-round core loop is playable start to finish with instant tap feedback and a
  scored reveal that names the model that fooled you most, when known.
- A passage bank exists and can be regenerated by a documented script/pipeline, dated by
  week, with human and AI passages paired and shuffled.
- The site is a single static build, deployable under a subpath, that plays correctly on
  desktop and mobile with no backend.
- You can share your score, and playing again gives you a different round from the
  current week's bank.
- The design direction in `docs/DESIGN.md` is fully executed — this ships as a designed
  game, not a functional stub.
